Uncanny Valley? More like a mountain!

Assignment: Post a written assessment to your blog of your thoughts and opinions on The Uncanny Valley (from the two videos we watched today). What is your take on hyperrealism? Do you feel like you identify with protagonists who are human-like or cartoonish? What are your thoughts on how CGI has advanced so much that artists have the ability to create our own realities? What are possible repercussions of CGI looking so realistic that it could be interpreted as reality? What do you feel are the responsibilities of artists who utilize these advanced CGI tools?

So, the Uncanny Valley. Is it creepy? Yes. That's the whole point. But, its getting better! Animation and robotics are both getting remarkably close to realistic human movement and expressions. Now, some people think that this is actually really scary and bad. Of course, in some senses, it is. We already have technology that can generate videos of actual people saying things that they have never said. For exampe, there is a video of Barrack Obama singing a song. Obviously, he never sung that song. Now, the scary part is that they can create videos of people saying very controversial things. However, I like to see the silver lining. Right now, there are many people that believe everything on the internet at face value. In the future, because of the realism of animation and AI, nothing can be trusted. The thing is, everybody thinks that we are in danger because their career will be ruined if there is a video of them saying something racist, generated by AI. But, if those videos are able to be created so easily, nobody will take them at face value. Therefore, people will actually stop blindly believing everything on the internet.

Aside from that, I think having hyperrealistic CGI is an amazing advancement, and is huge for animated movies and video games. Specifically for games, the more realistic a game can look (in many cases), at a good low cost, the more possibilities for immersion there are. And, immersion is key in gaming, especially in RPG's. So with the "ability to create our own realities" in hyper realistic graphics comes the new generation of games that feel so real, they may as well be live action movies you can control.

First Article: I think this article has the right idea, but the wrong opinion. I agree that virtual avatars and the worlds themselves do change the personalities and stuff of us, but in a good way. Usually, the avatars in game are free of consequence, and being able to do and say things without worrying about the effects does change how we act in real life-it makes us more aware of our actual actions.

Second Article: This article was really interesting, and made me think of some point that I have already thought about in the past. The same goes for games, if you're going to be putting yourself in place of the main character, why not make the main character a reflection of yourself, but enhanced with your best traits? Or in a more fantasy or sci fi setting, something that you wish you could be?

Assignment 03



Gender Accessebility in games, and online in general. Does it matter? Should it?
Well, after reading the article, I kind of agree and kind of disagree. I do agree that examples such as
Pokemon, where they ask "Are you a boy or a girl?" to determine the pronouns are out of date. However,
because of the advanced game making technology we have nowadays, its almost too easy to just have an input
field at the beginning of your game to enter your desired pronouns. So, while I agree that game makers should
provide this option to players, the fact that they're not makes me think there is some other reason it's not happening.
But in any case, there is not a single game in existance where your gender or sex or identity or anything actually matters.
Playing an avatar in a game is almost an extension of yourself, and games with robust character designer features really show that.
Other games are not about you, but about a certain character, in which case your desired preference doesn't matter because you're playing an already designed character. It's not supposed to be you. And in the examples of Pokemon, your character is only visible as a small pixelated thing, in games like that, most of the focus is on the Pokemon and not your own character.
Basically what I'm saying is that in my opinion, its a much simpler issue than people, and this article, are making it out to be.

All in all, I think this was pretty fun. The only gripe I have is that my background doesn't exactly match my lighting. But I think it's close enough!
